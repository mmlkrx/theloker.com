Possible Projects

All projects in this list still need to be defined in more detail. The initial effort per project should be 2-6 weeks with 4-16 hours per week depending on the type of project and available time.

  • Discover

    Imagine a website connected to the multiverse internet. This website would let you find a version of yourself that had the same problem that you have and provide you with the contact details of the person that solved that problem for them. Of course we don't have the multiverse internet (yet), so the goal would be to have a process, which helps find a coach/expert/trainer, that helped someone with a similar background/skill level/problem composition that you have, solve their problem.

  • Instamania

    A bot to organically grow engaged followers on instagram. Recently instagram has been restricting their public APIs, the goal of the bot would be to leverage the private API and completely escape detection by instagram by behaving in a natural manner (posting comments, liking, following, etc.).

  • Proof of Existence

    A simple website that allows people to submit any document or picture and store the data's SHA on the ethereum blockchain. Because the blockchain data can't be changed one can proof that a piece of data existed at a certain point in time. Should integrate with Metamask to make submitting the transaction easy.

  • Ghost Facebook

    Detailed instructions on how to download all of your data off of facebook and then delete everything and only keep a ghost profile on facebook to still be able to use messenger and events.

  • Fun Units

    A small tool for online magazine writers that need to convert arbitrary numbers into more visual units for their readers. I.e. 5.000 football fields, 7 times Manhatten island, 33.000 cans of coke, 100 elephants, etc.

  • Writescreen

    Screenplay writing software based on the fountain markdown format, backed by a git repository for version control. Integrates a process for writing exercises and organizing your notes and ideas around the story. Also provides a platform for publishing your work and getting early feedback as well as helping to submit finished scripts to production companies like Amazon Studios and Netflix.

  • Backstage

    Media management software for film productions. Media managers can determine what behind the scenes photos and footage is permitted to be released. Then crew and actors can access released photos and share with their social media following to help market the project and build anticipation. News websites can pay for early access.

  • Broadcast

    Broadcast helps anyone publish a high quality podcast. It provides best practices, makes sure podcasts are published on itunes, deploys a website for your podcast, helps you with transcribing podcast episodes, pitching sponsors, and recommends the right equipment as well as best practices and tools to use for remote interviewing guests etc.

  • Dolores

    A teachable agent you can train whenever you're learning something new. Dolores is a simple AI you can teach by building a mind map around core concepts. She will be able to take tests and answer questions about the topic you're teaching her. Teaching someone as a way to learn has been identified as very effective. Click here for a paper on teachable agents.

  • Grocery Alert

    Platform that lets people purchase supermarket groceries that are about to meet their date of expiry. Home delivery at night with bike messengers (potential cooperation with Foodora/Deliveroo). Supermarkets write off (throw away) food that is about to expire or just expired. People could buy these items on the platform.

  • Core Community

    How to create a space/community online that makes it easy/natural for members to engage with it. How to foster personal connections between community members. This would basically solve the problem of todays hyper connected but not really connected world. This core community would be a community where you would expect to meet members in real life.
